 When he finished placing his merchandise, 
the little man came out of the shop and stared in the window. 
He had placed the clocks and watches with great care – the clocks in a row at the back, 
and in front of them lying flat, a semicircle of watches. 
All the clocks had their faces neatlydivided in the middle by hands that pointed to six o’clock; 
all the watches, thin or fat, had their handspointing straight at three o’clock
